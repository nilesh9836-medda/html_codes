Spicy Garlic Shrimp Pasta

This Spicy Garlic Shrimp Pasta is a vibrant and flavorful dish that brings together tender shrimp, a kick of chili, and the aromatic warmth of garlic, all tossed with your favorite pasta. It's a fantastic option for a weeknight meal when you want something impressive yet easy, or for entertaining guests. The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity and the way the rich garlic-infused olive oil perfectly coats each strand of pasta and every succulent shrimp, making every bite a delight.


Ingredients

  • 8 oz (225g) Linguine, Spaghetti, or Fettuccine
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 1 lb (450g) large shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails on or off, your preference)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 - 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (like Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc) (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package directions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water before draining.
  2. Prepare the Shrimp: While the pasta is cooking, pat the shrimp dry with paper towels. In a medium bowl, toss the shrimp with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
  3. SautÃ© the Shrimp: Heat a large skillet or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the seasoned shrimp in a single layer (you may need to do this in two batches to avoid overcrowding). Cook for 1-2 minutes per side, until pink and opaque. Remove the cooked shrimp from the skillet and set aside
  4. Make the Sauce: In the same skillet, reduce the heat to medium. Add the remaining 3 tablespoons of olive oil, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes. SautÃ© for about 1 minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  5. Deglaze and Simmer: If using, pour in the white wine and let it simmer for 1-2 minutes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Stir in the chicken or vegetable broth and lemon juice. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer and let it reduce slightly for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Combine: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the sauce. Toss well to coat. Add the reserved pasta cooking water, a little at a time, until the sauce reaches your desired consistency.
  7. Finish: Stir in the cooked shrimp, fresh parsley, and the tablespoon of butter. Gently toss until everything is well combined and the butter has melted, creating a luscious sauce.
  8. Serve: Transfer the pasta to serving plates. Garnish with extra fresh parsley and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, if desired. Serve immediately.
